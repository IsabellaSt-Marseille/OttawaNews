Strange Facts About Ottawa

By Jim, Historian • Published January 2026 • Updated recently

1. Door color regulations in Kanata.
Certain neighborhoods in Kanata historically had rules about exterior door colors to maintain uniformity in housing developments. Source
2. Underground tunnels in downtown Ottawa.
Some office buildings and older hotels are connected via underground passages, primarily for convenience and safety during winter. Source
3. Cats on Parliament Hill.
In the 19th and early 20th century, cats were kept on Parliament Hill to help control rodent populations. Source
4. Whistling in public libraries.
While not a law, whistling in libraries is discouraged to maintain a quiet environment for readers. Source
Did you know? The Rideau Canal is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is used for both recreation and winter skating every year.
5. Rideau Canal treasure stories.
Local legends suggest that during early construction, treasure may have been hidden nearby. Historical records confirm the canal’s construction but not the treasure. Source
