Interesting Facts About Ottawa

By Jim, Historian • Published January 2026 • Updated recently

1. Ottawa’s Rideau Canal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The Rideau Canal, completed in 1832, is the oldest continuously operated canal system in North America and is recognized by UNESCO. Source
2. The “purple door law” in Kanata is a myth.
While some online sources claim you can’t paint your door purple in Kanata, this is not true. There are no such legal restrictions in the city. Source
3. Cats were historically used on Parliament Hill for pest control.
In the early 20th century, cats helped control rodents at government buildings. Today, this practice is no longer common. Source
4. Whistling in Ottawa libraries is discouraged but not illegal.
Libraries aim to maintain a quiet environment, so while there are no laws against whistling indoors, it is considered inappropriate. Source
Did you know? Every winter, the Rideau Canal transforms into the world’s largest naturally frozen skating rink!
5. Underground tunnels connect some downtown buildings.
Certain office towers and hotels in downtown Ottawa are linked via underground pathways for convenience during winter, but there are no “secret” tunnels for hidden treasure. Source
Fun Fact Sidebar:
Ottawa has over 30 museums and cultural institutions open to the public, including the Canadian Museum of History and the National Gallery.
