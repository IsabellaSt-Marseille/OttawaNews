Exterior Maintenance Overview

Policy overview provided by City of Ottawa – Housing Services
Published: January 2026
Last reviewed: January 2026

Purpose

This overview explains rules and guidelines for maintaining the exterior of residential properties in Ottawa. The goal is to ensure safe, clean, and well-maintained neighbourhoods for all residents.

Scope

These property standards apply to all residential properties within the City of Ottawa. They establish minimum expectations for exterior safety, cleanliness, and structural maintenance.

Exterior Maintenance Standards

Property owners are responsible for ensuring that the exterior of their buildings is safe and well-maintained. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Keeping walls, roofs, and foundations in good repair
  • Removing hazards such as broken glass or debris
  • Maintaining landscaping to prevent safety issues
  • Ensuring the property is generally clean and tidy

Decorative choices, such as paint colour or garden style, are not regulated under city-wide property standards unless otherwise specified by heritage or special agreements.

Exceptions and Special Cases

Certain properties may have additional requirements, including:

  • Designated heritage properties
  • Properties with specific development agreements
  • Homes governed by homeowners’ associations

Confirming Official Information

Residents are encouraged to consult official City of Ottawa by-laws or contact Housing Services to verify property standards and receive guidance.
