Property Standards and Exterior Appearance

Policy overview provided by City of Ottawa – Housing Services
Published: January 2026
Last reviewed: January 2026

Purpose

This policy overview explains how property standards by-laws apply to the exterior appearance of residential properties in Ottawa. The goal of these standards is to support safe, well-maintained neighbourhoods.

Scope

Property standards by-laws apply to residential properties within the City of Ottawa. These standards set minimum requirements related to safety and maintenance.

What Property Standards Address

Property standards by-laws may include requirements related to:

  • Structural condition of buildings
  • Maintenance of exterior walls and roofs
  • Removal of hazards and unsafe conditions
  • Basic cleanliness and upkeep

Exterior Appearance Guidelines

In general, property standards focus on safety and maintenance rather than decorative design choices.

Front Door Colour:
There is no city-wide property standards by-law that restricts the colour of a private home’s front door.

Residents may choose exterior colours freely unless additional guidelines apply to their property.

Exceptions and Special Cases

Additional appearance guidelines may apply to certain properties, including:

  • Designated heritage properties
  • Homes with specific development agreements
  • Properties governed by a homeowners’ association

These guidelines are not city-wide and apply only in specific situations.

Confirming Official Information

Residents are encouraged to review official City of Ottawa by-laws or contact Housing Services for the most current information.
