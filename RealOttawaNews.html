Property Appearance Rules in Ottawa

Written by: City of Ottawa – Housing Services
Published: January 2026
Reviewed and updated: January 2026

Purpose of Property Standards By-laws

Property standards by-laws set minimum requirements for maintaining residential buildings. These rules focus on safety, cleanliness, and general upkeep to support healthy communities.

What Property Standards By-laws Regulate

Property standards by-laws may regulate items such as:

  • Structural safety of buildings
  • Maintenance of exterior walls and roofs
  • Removal of hazards and debris
  • Basic sanitation and safety conditions

Exterior Appearance and Design

In general, property standards by-laws do not regulate decorative design choices. Exterior appearance rules focus on safety and maintenance rather than personal style.

Door Colour:
The City of Ottawa does not have a city-wide by-law that restricts the colour of a private home’s front door.

Homeowners may choose exterior colours freely unless additional rules apply to their property.

When Additional Rules May Apply

Some properties may be subject to additional appearance guidelines. These may include:

  • Designated heritage buildings
  • Homes governed by a homeowners’ association
  • Properties with special development agreements

These guidelines are not city-wide by-laws and only apply in specific situations.

Checking Official Information

Residents are encouraged to review official by-laws or contact the City of Ottawa to confirm current property standards requirements.
