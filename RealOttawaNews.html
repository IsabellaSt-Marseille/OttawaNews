Myth Check: Is It Illegal to Paint Your Door Purple in Ottawa?

Written by: City of Ottawa – Housing Services
Published: January 2026
Reviewed and updated: January 2026

You may have heard the claim that painting your front door purple is illegal in parts of Ottawa. This information has been shared online and through word of mouth, but it is important to check whether it is true.

Conclusion:
There is no city-wide law in Ottawa that makes it illegal to paint your front door purple.

The City of Ottawa does not have a by-law that restricts door colours for private homes. In most neighbourhoods, homeowners are free to choose the colour of their doors.

In some cases, special rules may apply to:

  • Heritage buildings
  • Homes with a homeowners’ association (HOA)
  • Buildings with specific design agreements

These rules are not city laws and only apply in specific situations. Residents can contact the City of Ottawa or review official by-laws if they are unsure.

City staff reviewed current Ottawa by-laws and housing guidelines to confirm this information. Sources include official city documents and public records.
